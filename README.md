# Trade with MAK: Founder-Led Program and Operations Case Study (2021-2023)

> **Status - historical portfolio case study:** This repository documents a subscription education and community program operated in 2021-2023. It was assembled in 2026 from retained business records and artifacts. It is not a live service, current market guidance, investment advice, or a claim of investment performance.

Trade with MAK was a paid education community for options day traders. This case study focuses on the program I helped build and operate: launch planning, acquisition, onboarding, curriculum, daily content operations, community systems, commerce, platform migration, privacy controls, and closeout.

## Timeline

- **Late 2021 - groundwork:** developed the brand, Discord structure, content templates, operating routines, and early social presence.
- **January 2022 - public launch:** subscriptions ran through LaunchPass and Stripe, which gated access to the Discord server. Launch work included an early-bird offer of $29.95 per month for the first three months against the standard $89.95 price, a launch-review contest, and a MacBook community contest.
- **2022 - program expansion:** added the MAK Trading School curriculum, daily recaps, weekly chart reviews, Sunday discussions, seasonal campaigns, and the historical 25K Challenge content concept documented in a companion repository.
- **Late 2022 - platform migration:** moved from LaunchPass to WordPress and MemberPress to gain greater control of billing, onboarding, pricing, and content delivery.
- **2023 - closeout:** wound down the community and the LLC rather than continuing an operating model that depended too heavily on one person's daily delivery.

## Program workstreams

### Community operations

The Discord environment included a trading floor, trade log, alert channels, options-flow and news feeds, bot integrations, and live voice access during market hours. I coordinated onboarding, administration, moderation, community contests, and recognition programs around that environment.

### Content and education operations

The recurring workflow covered premarket analysis, live-session commentary, time-stamped alerts, after-hours commentary documents, edited recap videos, weekly charts, and member-requested reviews. MAK Trading School added structured lessons in options basics, account/risk management, and annotated setups. Historical curriculum excerpts are preserved in the `mak-trading-school` repository with archival and safety context.

### Brand and acquisition

I directed the original `tradewithMAK` identity and the later 'Trade with the best' refresh. Campaigns included early-bird, launch-review, standard-join, MacBook, Black Friday, Christmas, and other seasonal promotions.

### Commerce and migration

The initial stack used LaunchPass and Stripe for payment and Discord access. The later WordPress/MemberPress stack increased control over billing, pricing experiments, coupon campaigns, onboarding, and content, but also introduced migration effort and churn risk.

## Operating decisions and controls

- **Cadence as a retention system:** recurring delivery was treated as an operating process rather than occasional creator content.
- **Education and process over outcome claims:** public materials should explain mechanics, decision discipline, and risk without promising results.
- **Privacy by design:** public artifacts exclude member names, handles, avatars, trade-account details, and account-level records.
- **Aggregate evidence only:** Stripe screenshots support business-scale claims but do not establish profitability or investment performance.
- **Closeout discipline:** the 2023 wind-down is included as part of the program lifecycle, not omitted from the story.

## What I learned

- Retention depends on reliable operating cadence, not acquisition campaigns alone.
- Founder-led delivery creates speed and authenticity but also key-person dependency.
- Owning the commerce stack improves control while increasing migration and support complexity.
- Metrics need a source, date, and scope; cumulative subscribers, active subscriptions, payments, and revenue are different measures.
- Community data creates privacy obligations that continue after a program ends.
